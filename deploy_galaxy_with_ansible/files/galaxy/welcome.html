Warning: at present, there are at most 152 threads, 2 TB memory space, and 100 TB disk space available in total for all users. To make sure that your and others' jobs can be run smoothly and successfully (i.e. ensure that storage and computing resources won't be depleted, which may cause the server to crash),

PLEASE:
  1. Run your pipeline as quickly as possible once you have uploaded your datasets, which will be deleted permanently after several weeks.
  2. Once your pipeline has been run successfully, you should download your results as quickly as possible. Job results will also be cleaned after a few days.
  3. There are a great deal of tools available from the Galaxy official repository. You can first search it to see whether some of them can satisfy your needs and then you can ask your admin to install them instead of developing them from scratch.
  4. Many tools do not expose full parameters to users. If needed and feasible, you can ask your admin to modify them.
  5. Galaxy training provides a lot of step-by-step tutorials for you. You can learn a lot from it from principle to practice. Including how to use Galaxy better, perform some concrete analyses as well as learn their underlying principles, etc.
  6. If you meet any problems, please contact your admin. Of course, you may Google it first!
  7. Some pipelines are really computing and storage-intensive (e.g. ENCODE bulk RNA-seq/ATAC-seq/ChIP-seq pipeline). If you intend to run those pipielines, please make sure that at present no one is running those pipelines and you should notify your admin and other members to avoid running those pipelines in time; Otherwise, your jobs may be broken down due to resource exhaustion.
  8. The above table will show those computing and storage-intensive jobs that are running at present. If the above table has listed many jobs, please avoid running any computing and storage-intensive tasks.
  9. You can visit NeuroBorder to see some cautions and steps of running some tools or analyses. If some analyses are frequently used by most people, please contact admin. If true and feasible, it's worth developing and deploying some tools at Galaxy and publishing some guides at NeuroBorder.
